About Tupac Shakur

Tupac Shakur, also known as 2Pac, was an American rapper, actor, and activist. He is one of the best-selling music artists of all time, known for his poetic and socially-conscious lyrics. 2Pac’s music explored issues like violence, racism, and life in inner cities.

Music

2Pac’s music is a blend of political, emotional, and street-savvy lyrics. Some of his most iconic albums include "All Eyez on Me," "Me Against the World," and "The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory." His songs like "Changes," "Dear Mama," and "California Love" continue to influence modern rap.

Legacy of Tupac

Despite his death in 1996, Tupac’s influence on hip-hop and popular culture remains profound. He shaped the way we view hip-hop as an art form and was pivotal in expressing the struggles of the black community. His activism and music still inspire artists and fans worldwide.

Hip-Hop Culture

Hip-Hop is more than just music. It's a movement that encompasses graffiti, dance (breaking), fashion, and DJing. It emerged in the 1970s in the Bronx, New York, and has grown to become a dominant force in global culture. Tupac was integral to this culture, shaping its growth in the 1990s.
